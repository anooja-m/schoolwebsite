Our Journey


The Faizal and Shabana Foundation in collaboration with the Government of Kerala developed the PRISM program to improve the standards of government schools through planned interventions. The Government Vocational Higher Secondary School for Girls, Nadakkavu in Kozhikode (Calicut) was the flagship school that was renovated as part of this program. The holistic makeover included a complete transformation in the built environment, with new classrooms, toilets, a multipurpose hall, interactive science center, astro-turf covered sports field, industrial size kitchen and library amongst other improvements.The school was ranked among top 3 government schools in India within 2 years of its refurbishment and is now seen as a model project for building schools across the nation.

About Us

The Government Vocational Higher Secondary School, Nadakkavu, Kozhikode is a girls school which has students primarily from lower income families of the region. In the current academic session, there are over 2,000 students. The PRISM (Promoting Regional Schools to International Standards through Multiple Intervention) project was implemented in this school in 2013.

Academics and co-curricular activities

The school offers high school education since 1942 and the vocational training sections was started in 1990. The clubs include an astronomy club and an entrepreneurship club. Also a career counselling program is organized for students.Also a career counselling program is organized for students.

Infrastructure and facilities

The school's 3-acre campus has its own indoor stadium with 3 basketball courts, an astro-turf-laden hockey ground, a football field and badminton courts.Additional infrastructure and facilities include a gym, a library, an atrium with a reading garden, and a dining hall with kitchen.
